How to Use DealFlow
Everything you need to know — in plain English.
Your Workflow — Step by Step
1
Get a listing from your broker
Your broker has properties on Crexi. Copy the listing description or paste the Crexi URL into the "Find Leads" tab.
2
Click "Run Full Pipeline"
AI reads the listing, figures out who the ideal buyer is, then searches LinkedIn, Apollo.io, Hunter.io, Yelp, Google Maps, Yellow Pages, Michigan SOS, Manta, BBB, and more across all Michigan cities. Returns 70 ranked leads instantly.
3
Review leads — highest score first
The number on the left is the AI Match Score (0–100). 80+ = hot lead, prioritize these. Read the "Why they match" section to understand exactly why this business would want this property.
4
Contact them — email or call
Click "Send Email" on any lead with an email address — it opens a pre-written, personalized email ready to send. Click "Call" to get a full phone script written specifically for that business. Or use "Send All" to blast everyone at once with automatic 3-step follow-ups.
5
Click "+ Save to Deal Tracker"
This moves your leads into the Deal Tracker tab, attached to that specific listing. You'll see them on a board where you can track each one through stages: Contacted → Responded → Showing → LOI → Under Contract → Closed.
6
Update stages as your broker works them
When a lead responds, moves to showing, or goes under contract — click the stage pill in Deal Tracker to update it. You always know exactly where every deal stands, and the commission forecast updates automatically.

What Do the Colored Badges Mean?
Every lead shows colored signal badges. These are reasons the AI thinks this business is likely to buy.
Red — Urgent
High-urgency signal. The business is actively frustrated, urgently needs space, or has publicly shown intent to buy. These are your hottest leads — contact same day.
Green — Growth
Growth signal. The business is expanding, has strong revenue, or is at the stage where buying makes financial sense. They have the ability and motivation to buy.
Yellow — Lease
Lease timing signal — this is extremely valuable. See explanation below.
Blue — New
New or recently registered business. Plant the seed now — they may not be ready today but will be in 2-3 years. Early contact = first-mover advantage.
Lease Signal
Why is this the most valuable signal?
Most commercial leases are 5 years long. When a lease expires, the business owner is forced to make a decision:

Re-sign — landlord usually raises rent 15–25%. They're at the mercy of someone else.
Move — disruptive, expensive, risky to lose patients/customers.
Buy — build equity, lock in their cost, never worry about eviction again.

This is your window. When their lease is expiring, they're already thinking about it. That's when they're most open to a conversation about buying instead of re-signing.
How does DealFlow detect this?
1. If the business opened in 2019–2021, their original 5-year lease is expiring right now or very soon.
2. If they posted on LinkedIn about lease costs, rent increases, or lease frustration — that's a direct buy signal.
3. If they're in a retail strip or office suite (not standalone building) — they're renting, not owning.

Not every lead has a lease signal — it only shows when there's actual evidence. A lead without this signal might still be interested, just harder to time perfectly.
What is the Deal Tracker?
The Deal Tracker is a visual board that tracks every lead for every listing through the stages of a real estate deal. Each listing gets its own separate board — you switch between them using the tab buttons at the top.
ContactedYou or your broker reached out — email or call sent
RespondedThey replied and showed interest — great sign, keep momentum
Showing ScheduledThey want to physically walk through the property
LOI SentLetter of Intent sent — a formal written offer. Deal is getting serious.
Under ContractThey signed a purchase agreement — nearly done, due diligence period
ClosedDeal is done. Commission is earned. You get paid.
Save to Deal Tracker — after running leads, click this button on the leads page and your leads get automatically attached to that listing's board. Then switch to Deal Tracker and move them through stages as your broker works them.
What is the CSV Export?
CSV is a spreadsheet file. When you click "↓ CSV", it downloads all your leads as a file that opens in Excel or Google Sheets — like what you saw in the screenshot with all the columns (Score, Name, Email, Signals, Why Match, etc.).

Use it to: share the lead list with your broker, keep a personal record, or filter/sort in Excel. The file is named dealflow_leads.csv and opens directly in Excel.
